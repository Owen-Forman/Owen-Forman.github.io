What Is It?

Cryptography is the art of writing or solving codes, and has been around for centuries. Military leaders new and old have used cryptography to send coded/hidden messages withought fear of them being intercepted. Continue reading to learn some basic, and a few more advanced methods of cryptography.

Codes and Ciphers

Here are a few basic and intermediate codes and ciphers that give you

Caesar Cipher

A fairly simple cipher, where a letter is shifted a certaint amount of spaces up the alphabet

Vigenère cipher

The Vigenère cipher uses a series of interwoven Caesar ciphers based on the letters of a keyword to encode a messge.

Base 64

Much like Binary, it's a representation of data, but can also be used to encrytp data.

Binary

A way of representing data, can be used to hide basic messages.

Morse Code

Written down in -'s and .'s, it is used to transmit messages. Can also be hidden as sound

Skip/Skip Cipher

A cipher that requires the decoder to skip X number of letters to find each letter in the message

Substition Cipher

A cipher that requires the decoder to substitute letters or numbers with other letters and numbers

Numbers To Letters

A very common type of substition cipher, that replaces a with 1, b with 2, and so on. Can also be used in reverse to go from letters to numbers.
